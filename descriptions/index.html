Chapter Selection

Descriptions

Here you'll find descriptions of the exquisite lands and creatures appearing throughout Kiklos' worlds.

Cyclop

Cyclops are one of the beasts responsible for maintaining the connection between the creatures and the spirits. Being isolated primates, they have developed an extraordinary sense of communication with nature, instead of with themselves. They barely have any spoken language, although they are fluent in chanting spells to awaken and influence nature's spirits. A cyclop can have a lifespan of over 500 years, and an infant stage of over 30 years. Cyclops usually invest their knowledge and time into befriending specific elemental spirits, but some choose to master more than one kind of element.

Sunfox

A sunfox is a fire-breathing predator native to the Vermillion Plains. Usually has yellow fur, and a small size. They typically prey on crane-eggs.

Long-neck Crane

A very large bird with a retractable neck. Extremely keen and protective of their nests. They are intelligent animals and tend to be kind with spiritual beasts.

Mushrooms

The bodies of myscelium nets that are connected to each other under pretty much any biome. The mushrooms may or may not be connected with their myscelium nets spiritually: it depends on their species. This means mushrooms can act either as a hive or as an organism. Mushrooms, hive-like or not, can act on both sides of the spectrum: in favor or against the balance of nature.

Blue Mushrooms

Hive-like mushrooms native to the Powerful Rainforests, they act as an allie for the natural spirits.

Earth Spirits

Completely loyal spirits, they are capable of giving you shelter, food, and/or safety for their animal allies.

Felines

Feline spirits are one of the most active elemental earth spirits. They will provide you shelter and defense force. Once a feline spirit befriends a mortal, he will be loyal to that bloodline for as long as the animal proves himself as an allie of the Earth.

The Vermillion Plains

Large expansions of red-grasslands, ponds, and habitat to an extensive fauna and flora.
